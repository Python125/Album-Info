Pet Sounds

No one is educated musically 'til they've heard [Pet Sounds] - Paul McCartney

Pet Sounds is the eleventh studio album by the American rock band the Beach Boys, released on May 16, 1966 by Capitol Records. It was initially met with a lukewarm critical and commercial response in the United States, peaking at number 10 on the Billboard Top LPs chart. In the United Kingdom, the album was lauded by critics and reached number 2 on the Record Retailer chart, remaining in the top ten for six months. Promoted there as "the most progressive pop album ever", Pet Sounds was recognized for its ambitious production, sophisticated music, and emotional lyrics.

Wouldn't It Be Nice

"Wouldn't It Be Nice" is a classic song by the American rock band the Beach Boys. It was written by Brian Wilson, Tony Asher, and Mike Love, and released as the opening track on their iconic album "Pet Sounds" in 1966. The song is known for its upbeat and catchy melody, as well as its optimistic lyrics about a couple dreaming of being together and living their lives to the fullest. The song features layered harmonies, complex arrangements, and the use of non-musical instruments, such as bicycle bells and dog whistles, to add texture and depth to the sound.

Other Trivia

The instrumental for "Sloop John B" was first recorded on July 12, 1965 and it took fourteen takes to complete. Brian and Mike's vocals were recorded on December 22, 1965.

During the the Pet Sounds sessions, Brian began his first recording session for "Good Vibrations" on February 17, 1966.

While making Pet Sounds, Brian created many mono mixes that differentiated from the released mono mixes on the album. Many of these alternate mixes can be heard on disc 3 of The Pet Sounds Sessions box set.
